# LuvOnPurpose System - Competitive Salary Recommendations

## Market Research Summary (January 2026)

Based on current market data from BLS, Glassdoor, Salary.com, Indeed, and nonprofit compensation reports, the following salary ranges are recommended to attract and retain highly qualified managers.

---

## Executive Leadership Positions

### Chief Financial Officer (CFO) / Financial Controller
**Market Data:**
- Small nonprofit ($1M budget): $80,000 - $120,000
- Nonprofit CFO average: $150,000 - $287,000
- Financial Controller: $120,000 - $210,000

**Recommended Range:** $95,000 - $145,000
**Justification:** For a growing multi-entity organization, this range is competitive for attracting experienced financial professionals while remaining sustainable for a nonprofit structure.

---

### Executive Director / CEO (The L.A.W.S. Collective)
**Market Data:**
- Nonprofit Executive Director (national): $112,000 - $186,000
- Atlanta nonprofit ED: $97,000 - $113,000
- Candid 2024 report: $115,000 - $235,000

**Recommended Range:** $110,000 - $165,000
**Justification:** As the umbrella organization overseeing multiple entities, this position requires strategic leadership experience and should be compensated at the higher end of nonprofit ED ranges.

---

## Director-Level Positions

### Education/Training Director (Academy)
**Market Data:**
- Training and Development Manager (BLS median): $127,090
- Director of Training (Glassdoor): $147,113
- Education Director: $73,000 - $140,000

**Recommended Range:** $85,000 - $125,000
**Justification:** Given the requirement for advanced degrees (Masters in Education, Masters in Criminal Justice), this range reflects the specialized expertise needed. Cornelius's dual master's degrees warrant compensation at the higher end.

---

### LLC Formation Director / Trust Systems Director
**Market Data:**
- Program Director, Nonprofit: $68,000 - $140,000
- Business Development Director: $169,661 average
- Legal/Compliance Director: $120,000 - $180,000

**Recommended Range:** $80,000 - $120,000
**Justification:** These specialized roles require knowledge of entity formation, trust structures, and legal compliance. The range accounts for the unique expertise required.

---

### Marketing Director
**Market Data:**
- Marketing Director (national): $118,000 - $176,000
- Small business Marketing Director: $93,000 - $170,000
- Nonprofit Marketing: $75,000 - $110,000

**Recommended Range:** $75,000 - $110,000
**Justification:** Marketing in the nonprofit/social enterprise space typically pays less than corporate, but this range is competitive for attracting experienced marketers who align with the mission.

---

### Technology Director / IT Director
**Market Data:**
- IT Director (nonprofit): $77,000 - $140,000
- Director of Information Technology: $130,000 average
- Nonprofit IT: $85,000 - $120,000

**Recommended Range:** $80,000 - $115,000
**Justification:** Technology leadership is critical for the automation platform. This range attracts qualified candidates while remaining within nonprofit norms.

## Manager-Level Positions

### Operations Manager
**Market Data:**
- Operations Manager (national): $63,000 - $106,000
- Augusta, GA Manager: $80,000 - $145,000
- Operations Manager (Glassdoor): $81,000 - $118,000

**Recommended Range:** $65,000 - $95,000
**Justification:** Operations management in Georgia typically falls below national averages. This range is competitive for the Augusta/Atlanta market.

---

### Program Managers (Various)
**Market Data:**
- Program Director, Nonprofit: $62,000 - $90,000 (Atlanta)
- Program Manager: $70,000 - $95,000
- Nonprofit Program Director: $78,000 average

**Recommended Range:** $60,000 - $85,000
**Justification:** Program managers overseeing specific initiatives (grants, outreach, community programs) should be compensated competitively within nonprofit norms.

## Benefits Package Recommendations

To remain competitive, salary should be supplemented with:

1. **Health Insurance** - Medical, dental, vision (employer pays 70-80%)
2. **Retirement** - 401(k) or 403(b) with 3-5% employer match
3. **PTO** - 15-20 days annually + holidays
4. **Professional Development** - $2,000-$5,000 annual budget
5. **Flexible Work** - Remote/hybrid options where applicable
6. **Performance Bonuses** - 5-15% of base salary for meeting goals

---

## Georgia Cost of Living Adjustment

Georgia (particularly Augusta) has a lower cost of living than national averages:
- Augusta cost of living index: 89.4 (national = 100)
- Atlanta cost of living index: 102.4

The recommended ranges account for this, positioning salaries to be competitive locally while remaining sustainable for the organization.

---

## Funding Considerations

For a multi-entity nonprofit structure, these salaries can be funded through:
1. **Grant allocations** - Many grants allow up to 15-20% for administrative/personnel costs
2. **Program revenue** - Academy tuition, consulting fees
3. **Entity allocation** - Each entity contributes proportionally to shared leadership costs
4. **Phased implementation** - Start at entry range, increase as revenue grows
