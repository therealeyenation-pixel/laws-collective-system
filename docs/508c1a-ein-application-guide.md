# 508(c)(1)(A) EIN Application Guide

## LuvOnPurpose Outreach Temple and Academy Society

This guide covers the EIN application process for a 508(c)(1)(A) religious/educational organization.

---

## What is a 508(c)(1)(A)?

A 508(c)(1)(A) organization is a **mandatory exception** from 501(c)(3) requirements for churches and religious organizations. Unlike 501(c)(3) organizations:

- **No IRS application required** for tax-exempt status (automatic)
- **No annual Form 990 filing** required
- **Donations are tax-deductible** for donors
- **Greater privacy** - less public disclosure required
- **Protected by First Amendment** religious freedom

---

## EIN Application Differences

When applying for an EIN for a 508(c)(1)(A), the process differs from LLCs:

### Organization Type Selection

On the IRS EIN application, select:
- **Type of Entity:** "Church or church-controlled organization"
- OR "Other nonprofit/tax-exempt organization"

### Key Questions

| Question | Answer for 508(c)(1)(A) |
|----------|------------------------|
| Type of entity | Church or church-controlled organization |
| Reason for applying | Started a new business/organization |
| Principal activity | Religious activities / Educational activities |
| Products/Services | Religious education, spiritual development, community outreach |

### Step 2: Apply Online
1. Go to [IRS EIN Assistant](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online)
2. Select "View Additional Types, Including Tax-Exempt and Governmental Organizations"
3. Select "Church or Church-Controlled Organization"
4. Complete the application

### Step 3: Receive EIN
- EIN is issued immediately upon successful online application
- Save the CP 575 notice for your records

---

## Important Notes

### No 501(c)(3) Application Needed
Unlike other nonprofits, 508(c)(1)(A) organizations do **NOT** need to file Form 1023 or 1023-EZ. Tax-exempt status is automatic under IRC Section 508(c)(1)(A).

### State Requirements
While federal tax-exempt status is automatic, you may still need to:
- Register with your state's Secretary of State
- Apply for state tax exemption separately
- Register for charitable solicitation (if applicable)

### Maintaining Status
To maintain 508(c)(1)(A) status:
- Operate exclusively for religious/educational purposes
- Maintain religious character and activities
- Keep financial records (though not required to file publicly)
- Avoid private benefit/inurement

## Questions to Expect

The IRS online application will ask:

1. **What type of legal structure?** → Church or church-controlled organization
2. **Why are you applying?** → Started a new organization
3. **What is the principal activity?** → Religious/Educational
4. **What products/services?** → Religious education and community outreach
5. **Do you have employees?** → No (unless you do)
6. **Do you expect to pay wages?** → No (unless you do)
