## ARTICLE II: THE 60/40 STRUCTURE

### Section 2.1 - Constitutional Allocation
All Trust assets, income, and appreciation shall be allocated as follows:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    TOTAL TRUST VALUE                        │
├─────────────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────┤
│      HOUSE RETAINED (60%)       │    NETWORK POOL (40%)     │
│                                 │                           │
│  ✓ Protected Family Wealth      │  ✓ Partnership Capital    │
│  ✓ Generational Inheritance     │  ✓ Investment Funds       │
│  ✓ Emergency Reserve            │  ✓ Business Ventures      │
│  ✓ Education Fund               │  ✓ Strategic Alliances    │
│                                 │                           │
│  NEVER accessible to outside    │  Available for approved   │
│  parties under any circumstance │  external relationships   │
└─────────────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────┘
```

### Section 2.2 - House Retained Portion (60%)
The House Retained portion shall be used exclusively for:
1. Direct benefit of Beneficiary and their descendants
2. Educational expenses for family members
3. Healthcare and emergency needs
4. Housing and basic living expenses
5. Generational wealth preservation

### Section 2.3 - Network Pool Portion (40%)
The Network Pool portion may be used for:
1. Business investments and partnerships
2. Revenue-sharing arrangements
3. Strategic alliances with approved partners
4. Community and philanthropic initiatives
5. Growth capital for House enterprises

### Section 2.4 - Firewall Protection
The 60% House Retained portion is ABSOLUTELY PROTECTED:
- No investor, partner, or creditor may access this portion
- No contract or agreement may encumber this portion
- This protection survives bankruptcy, divorce, or legal judgment
- Only the House Principal may authorize distributions from this portion

## ARTICLE III: BENEFICIARY RIGHTS

### Section 3.1 - Income Distributions
The Beneficiary shall receive distributions according to the following schedule:

| Distribution Type | Frequency | Source | Approval Required |
|-------------------|-----------|--------|-------------------|
| Living Expenses | Monthly | House Retained | Trustee |
| Education Funding | As Needed | House Retained | Trustee |
| Emergency Funds | As Needed | House Retained | Trustee + Principal |
| Profit Sharing | Quarterly | Network Pool | House Council |
| Special Distributions | Annual | Either | Principal |

### Section 3.2 - Principal Distributions
The Beneficiary may request principal distributions for:
1. Purchase of primary residence
2. Starting an approved business
3. Major medical expenses
4. Educational advancement
5. Other purposes approved by the House Principal

### Section 3.3 - Restrictions
The Beneficiary may NOT:
1. Assign, pledge, or encumber their beneficial interest
2. Use Trust assets as collateral for personal debts
3. Transfer rights to non-family members
4. Access the 60% House Retained portion without Principal approval

---

## ARTICLE IV: TRUSTEE DUTIES

### Section 4.1 - Fiduciary Responsibilities
The Trustee shall:
1. Manage Trust assets prudently and in good faith
2. Maintain accurate records of all transactions
3. Provide quarterly reports to the Beneficiary and House Principal
4. Invest Trust assets according to the House Investment Policy
5. Distribute income and principal as directed by this Agreement

### Section 4.2 - Investment Standards
The Trustee shall invest Trust assets according to the following standards:
- Preservation of capital as primary objective
- Diversification across asset classes
- Preference for income-generating investments
- Avoidance of speculative or high-risk investments
- Alignment with House values and ethics

### Section 4.3 - Compensation
The Trustee shall receive compensation of {{TRUSTEE_COMPENSATION_PERCENT}}% of Trust assets annually, payable from Trust income.

## ARTICLE VI: TRUST ADMINISTRATION

### Section 6.1 - Accounting
The Trustee shall maintain complete and accurate records including:
- All receipts and disbursements
- Investment transactions
- Asset valuations
- Distribution records
- Tax filings

### Section 6.2 - Reporting
The Trustee shall provide:
- Monthly statements to the Beneficiary
- Quarterly reports to the House Principal
- Annual accountings to all interested parties
- Tax information as required by law

### Section 6.3 - Successor Trustee
If the Trustee is unable or unwilling to serve:
1. The House Principal shall appoint a successor
2. If no appointment is made within 60 days, the House Council shall appoint
3. The successor Trustee shall have all powers of the original Trustee

---

## ARTICLE VII: SPENDTHRIFT PROVISIONS

### Section 7.1 - Protection from Creditors
No beneficiary's interest in the Trust may be:
- Assigned, alienated, or transferred
- Subject to attachment, garnishment, or execution
- Reached by any creditor of the beneficiary
- Included in the beneficiary's bankruptcy estate

### Section 7.2 - Exception for Support Obligations
Notwithstanding Section 7.1, the Trust may be subject to orders for:
- Child support
- Spousal support
- As required by applicable law

---

## ARTICLE VIII: AMENDMENT AND TERMINATION

### Section 8.1 - Amendment
This Agreement may be amended only by:
1. Written consent of the Grantor (if living)
2. Approval of the House Principal
3. Consent of the Trustee
4. Notice to all beneficiaries

### Section 8.2 - Protected Provisions
The following provisions may NEVER be amended:
- Article II (The 60/40 Structure)
- Article VII (Spendthrift Provisions)
- This Section 8.2

### Section 8.3 - Termination
The Trust shall terminate upon:
1. Distribution of all Trust assets
2. Death of all beneficiaries without successors
3. Unanimous agreement of all parties (excluding protected provisions)
