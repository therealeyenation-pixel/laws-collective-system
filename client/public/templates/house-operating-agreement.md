## ARTICLE III: DECISION-MAKING PROCEDURES

### Section 3.1 - Decision Categories

| Category | Examples | Approval Required |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Routine** | Minor expenses, scheduling | Steward alone |
| **Operational** | Events, programs, budgets | Steward + Treasurer |
| **Significant** | Major purchases, new members | Council majority |
| **Strategic** | Investments, partnerships | Council 2/3 + Principal |
| **Constitutional** | Charter amendments | Unanimous + Principal |

### Section 3.2 - Voting Weights

| Position | Voting Weight | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| House Principal | 3x | Plus veto power |
| House Steward | 2x | |
| House Treasurer | 1x | |
| Council Members | 1x each | |
| General Members | 0x | Voice but no vote |

### Section 3.3 - Quorum Requirements
- **Council Meetings:** Majority of Council members
- **General Meetings:** 25% of all members
- **Special Meetings:** As specified in meeting notice

### Section 3.4 - Principal Veto
The House Principal may veto any decision by:
1. Declaring the veto within 7 days of the decision
2. Providing written explanation to the Council
3. The veto is final and not subject to override

## ARTICLE IV: FINANCIAL OPERATIONS

### Section 4.1 - Fiscal Year
The House fiscal year shall run from {{FISCAL_YEAR_START}} to {{FISCAL_YEAR_END}}.

### Section 4.2 - Budget Process
1. Treasurer prepares draft budget (60 days before fiscal year)
2. Council reviews and recommends changes (30 days before)
3. Principal approves final budget (15 days before)
4. Budget takes effect at start of fiscal year

## ARTICLE V: MEMBER RIGHTS AND RESPONSIBILITIES

### Section 5.1 - Member Rights
All members in good standing have the right to:

**Participation Rights:**
- Attend all general meetings
- Voice opinions on any matter
- Access House facilities and resources
- Receive financial distributions
- Review non-confidential records

**Information Rights:**
- Receive meeting notices
- Access financial statements
- Review operating policies
- Obtain copies of governing documents

**Appeal Rights:**
- Challenge decisions affecting them
- Request Council review
- Appeal to Principal for final decision

### Section 5.2 - Member Responsibilities
All members are expected to:

**Conduct:**
- Uphold House values and reputation
- Treat other members with respect
- Maintain confidentiality of House matters
- Participate in House activities

**Financial:**
- Pay any required contributions on time
- Report changes in financial status
- Use House resources responsibly

**Communication:**
- Respond to official communications
- Update contact information
- Attend required meetings

### Section 5.3 - Good Standing Requirements
A member is in good standing if they:
1. Have no outstanding financial obligations
2. Have not been subject to disciplinary action
3. Have attended at least {{MIN_ATTENDANCE_PERCENT}}% of required meetings
4. Have fulfilled any assigned responsibilities

### Section 5.4 - Disciplinary Procedures

| Offense Level | Examples | Consequence |
|---------------|----------|-------------|
| Minor | Missed meetings, late payments | Warning |
| Moderate | Repeated minor offenses | Probation |
| Serious | Breach of confidentiality | Suspension |
| Severe | Fraud, theft, harm to House | Expulsion |

**Process:**
1. Written notice of alleged offense
2. Opportunity to respond (14 days)
3. Council review and recommendation
4. Principal decision (final)

## ARTICLE VI: MEETINGS AND VOTING

### Section 6.1 - Meeting Types

| Meeting Type | Frequency | Attendees | Purpose |
|--------------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Council | Monthly | Council only | Governance |
| General | Quarterly | All members | Updates, voting |
| Annual | Yearly | All members | Elections, reports |
| Special | As needed | As specified | Urgent matters |
| Emergency | Rare | Council + Principal | Crisis response |

### Section 6.2 - Meeting Notice
- **Regular Meetings:** 14 days advance notice
- **Special Meetings:** 7 days advance notice
- **Emergency Meetings:** 24 hours notice (or as soon as practical)

### Section 6.3 - Meeting Conduct
1. Principal or designee presides
2. Secretary records minutes
3. Agenda distributed in advance
4. Robert's Rules of Order apply
5. All members may speak (time limits apply)

### Section 6.4 - Voting Procedures
- **Voice Vote:** For routine matters
- **Show of Hands:** For significant matters
- **Written Ballot:** For elections and sensitive matters
- **Proxy Voting:** Allowed with written authorization

### Section 6.5 - Remote Participation
Members may participate remotely via:
- Video conference
- Telephone
- Written proxy

Remote participants count toward quorum and may vote.

## ARTICLE VII: CONFLICT RESOLUTION

### Section 7.1 - Informal Resolution
Members are encouraged to resolve disputes informally through:
1. Direct communication
2. Mediation by neutral member
3. Steward facilitation

### Section 7.2 - Formal Grievance Process

**Step 1: Written Complaint**
- Submit to Steward within 30 days of incident
- Include all relevant facts and requested resolution

**Step 2: Investigation**
- Steward investigates within 14 days
- Both parties interviewed
- Evidence gathered

**Step 3: Council Review**
- Council reviews within 14 days
- Both parties may present
- Council recommends resolution

**Step 4: Principal Decision**
- Principal reviews within 7 days
- Decision is final and binding

### Section 7.3 - Confidentiality
All grievance proceedings are confidential. Breach of confidentiality is a serious offense.

### Section 8.1 - Required Records
The House shall maintain:
- House Charter and amendments
- Operating Agreement and amendments

- Member records

- Correspondence

### Section 8.2 - Record Access
- **Public Records:** Available to all members
- **Confidential Records:** Council and Principal only
- **Restricted Records:** Principal only

### Section 8.3 - Record Retention

| Record Type | Retention Period |
|-------------|------------------|
| Charter/Agreements | Permanent |
| Meeting Minutes | Permanent |
| Financial Records | 7 years |
| Tax Records | 7 years |
| Member Records | Duration + 3 years |
| Correspondence | 3 years |

## ARTICLE IX: AMENDMENTS

### Section 9.1 - Amendment Process
1. Proposal submitted in writing
2. Council review (30 days)
3. Member notification (14 days before vote)
4. Council vote (2/3 majority required)
5. Principal approval
6. Effective 30 days after approval

### Section 9.2 - Protected Provisions
The following may not be amended without House Charter amendment:
- 60/40 allocation structure
- Principal veto power
- Firewall protections
- Spendthrift provisions
